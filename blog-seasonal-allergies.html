Seasonal Allergies: Prevention and Treatment

April 28, 2023 4 min read By Dr. Peter Kamau

Seasonal allergies, also known as hay fever or allergic rhinitis, affect millions of people worldwide. In Kenya, pollen from various trees, grasses, and weeds can trigger uncomfortable symptoms during different seasons. Understanding prevention and treatment options can help you manage these allergies effectively.

Identifying Seasonal Allergies

Seasonal allergies occur when your immune system overreacts to outdoor allergens like pollen. Common symptoms include:

  • Sneezing
  • Runny or stuffy nose
  • Itchy or watery eyes
  • Itchy throat or ears
  • Postnasal drip
  • Fatigue (from poor sleep due to congestion)

Did You Know?

In Kenya, pollen counts tend to be highest during dry seasons when plants release pollen into the air. The long rains (March-May) and short rains (October-December) can temporarily reduce pollen levels.

Common Allergy Triggers in Kenya

Different plants pollinate at different times of year:

Season Common Triggers
January - March Grass pollens, some tree pollens
April - June Mold spores (increased during rains)
July - September Weed pollens, grass pollens
October - December Mold spores, some weed pollens

Prevention Strategies

While you can't completely avoid allergens, these strategies can help reduce exposure:

1. Monitor Pollen Counts

Check local weather reports for pollen forecasts. On high pollen days:

  • Stay indoors during peak pollen times (usually morning)
  • Keep windows closed at home and in your car
  • Use air conditioning with HEPA filters

2. Personal Protection

  • Wear sunglasses to protect your eyes from pollen
  • Consider wearing a mask when doing outdoor chores
  • Shower after being outdoors to rinse pollen from your skin and hair
  • Change clothes after spending time outside

3. Home Environment

  • Use high-efficiency filters in your vacuum and air conditioner
  • Wash bedding weekly in hot water
  • Keep indoor humidity below 50% to prevent mold growth
  • Remove shoes at the door to avoid tracking in pollen

"Many patients don't realize that starting allergy medications before symptoms begin can provide better control throughout the season."

- Dr. Peter Kamau, Allergy Specialist

Treatment Options

Several effective treatments are available at Larkpharm:

1. Over-the-Counter Medications

  • Antihistamines: Cetirizine, Loratadine, Fexofenadine (relieve sneezing, itching, runny nose)
  • Decongestants: Pseudoephedrine, Phenylephrine (reduce nasal congestion)
  • Nasal sprays: Cromolyn sodium (prevents allergic reactions), saline sprays (moisturize nasal passages)
  • Eye drops: Ketotifen (relieves itchy, watery eyes)

2. Prescription Treatments

For more severe cases, your doctor may recommend:

  • Nasal corticosteroids: Fluticasone, Mometasone (reduce inflammation)
  • Leukotriene modifiers: Montelukast (blocks allergy-causing chemicals)
  • Allergy shots (immunotherapy): Long-term treatment that reduces sensitivity to allergens

3. Natural Remedies

Some people find relief with:

  • Saline nasal irrigation (neti pot)
  • Butterbur extract (may have antihistamine properties)
  • Quercetin (a natural antioxidant found in some foods)
  • Local honey (evidence is limited but some patients report benefits)

When to See a Doctor

Consult a healthcare provider if:

  • OTC medications aren't providing relief
  • You experience wheezing or shortness of breath
  • Symptoms last longer than two weeks
  • You develop sinus pain or thick yellow/green nasal discharge

Final Thoughts

Seasonal allergies can significantly impact quality of life, but with proper prevention and treatment, most people can find relief. At Larkpharm, our pharmacists can help you choose the right medications and develop a personalized allergy management plan. Remember that starting treatment before allergy season begins can often provide better symptom control.
